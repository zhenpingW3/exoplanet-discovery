📈 Discovery Timeline: How Fast Are We Finding?

The number of discovered exoplanets has accelerated dramatically. Most discoveries come from specific detection methods that bias us toward certain planet types.

💡 Insight: 95% of all exoplanets were discovered after 2009. The Transit method dominates (~96%), detecting planets that pass in front of their stars.

🪐 Planetary Types: What Have We Found?

Planets come in many types, far more diverse than our solar system. Click on a type to see examples.

💡 Insight: Hot Jupiters (massive planets orbiting extremely close to their stars) are overrepresented in our sample. This is because they're easy to detect—not because they're common!
